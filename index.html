I would say that my greatest skill is my adaptability. I have developed this skill by participating in a wide variety of extracurriculars from athletics to performing arts as well as enrolling in AP classes. Racing against other schools on the track and field was unnerving. Performing on stage in front of hundreds as a violist was discouraging. Executing rifle drills while a brigade watched was daunting. But from all these initially unpleasant experiences, I learned how to improve upon myself by developing characteristics and habits that ultimately made me adaptable. To me, this means being able to perform tasks with proficiency and finesse no matter what they may be. The situation that most profoundly exhibited this trait was during a track meet against three other schools. The bleachers were filled with students of all ages, eagerly cheering their friends and classmates on. As a 100 meter sprinter myself, running the 400 meter race was not ideal for me, but I trained in the little time I had to prepare for it. I ran up and down hills early in the morning before school to condition my body for endurance. Though it was only for a small portion of an hour, every minute I took to get ready for the race made a big difference. After stepping up to the starting line I felt a surge of confidence and my mind cleared of all distractions, trusting that my body will cooperate. I ended up taking second place.
